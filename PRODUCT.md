# PRODUCT.md — PreOp

## Register

product

(The interface serves the patient watching the video. Marketing comes later.)

## Users

**Primary: the patient.** Someone with a surgery scheduled in the next days or weeks. Anxious, lying awake at 11pm searching their diagnosis on their phone. Not medically trained. Wants to *understand* what is about to happen to their body, in language and visuals they can absorb.

**Secondary: their family member.** Same anxiety, fewer details, often the one driving the patient to the hospital and helping them prepare.

**Tertiary: the surgeon or clinic.** Will eventually distribute these videos to their patients; they care that the content is anatomically accurate and medically defensible.

The patient drives every design decision. If a frame, word, or interaction would not help an anxious 11pm phone user, it does not exist.

## Product Purpose

PreOp generates a personalized two-minute cinematic video for a single patient about their specific upcoming surgery. The patient enters a freeform diagnosis or uploads their medical report; thirty seconds later they have a vertical film that walks them through their body, the problem, the procedure, and the healed result. It replaces the generic, fifty-thousand-dollar stock animation that hospitals show every patient regardless of their case.

## Brand and Tone

**Calm. Anatomical. Specific.** This is a healthcare product but it is not a hospital app, and it is not a wellness app either. It does not chirp at you. It does not say "let's get started!" It does not show stock photos of smiling diverse hands holding pill bottles.

The voice is that of a thoughtful surgeon who has time for you. Plain language. Short sentences. Quiet confidence. Never clinical jargon, never therapy-app warmth.

The visual identity is closer to an arthouse short film than a SaaS dashboard. Vertical orientation. Anatomical-narrative imagery. Restrained motion. The product disappears so the content can do its work.

## Anti-references

- **No hospital UI.** No teal-and-white sterile chrome, no clipboard icons, no "Patient #" labels.
- **No wellness app aesthetic.** No gradients of mint and lavender, no rounded mascots, no "you've got this!"
- **No SaaS dashboard.** No metric tiles, no charts, no sidebars, no settings, no breadcrumbs.
- **No medical drama.** No blood, no scary instruments, no surgical theater photography.
- **No AI app reflex.** No "✨ AI is thinking" sparkles, no purple-blue gradients, no chatbot bubbles.

## Strategic Principles

1. **The patient is anonymous in every frame.** No identifiable face, ever. The patient projects themselves onto the figure.
2. **One surgery, perfectly demoed, beats four surgeries half-working.** Hackathon scope is arthroscopic meniscus repair. Polish the one path before generalizing.
3. **Education first, emotion second.** Calm-app aesthetics did not teach the patient anything. The product is anatomical-narrative: problem, procedure, outcome. Emotion comes from clarity.
4. **Every frame earns its runtime.** If a frame could be a slide, it should not be a video. Motion must reveal something a static diagram cannot.
5. **The product is the video. Everything else is distraction.** No accounts, no settings, no history, no analytics, no sharing dashboards. One input, one button, one player.
6. **The frontend is the last priority and the highest ceiling.** A working pipeline with a flat UI loses to a working pipeline with a beautiful one. The player itself must feel premium.

## The pitch frame

PreOp is the layer between the diagnosis and the surgery that has been missing for fifty years of modern medicine. The cost curve on personalized visual content has just bent; we are the product that bends with it.
